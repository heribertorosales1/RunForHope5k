The brothers of Pi Kappa Alpha and Pi Kappa Phi invite you to join the Run for Hope 5K, a philanthropy event dedicated to mental health and suicide prevention.

Funds raised will help support Active Minds’ student-led mental health programs, campus initiatives, educational outreach, and resources for students and young adults. Whether you run, walk, sponsor, or donate, you’re helping bring hope to our community.

Race Route

The Run for Hope 5K follows the University of Alabama’s official 5K loop and starts and finishes at the UA Student Recreation Center.

This 3.1-mile course loops through campus with clearly marked directions and volunteers stationed throughout the route. Walkers, runners, and strollers are welcome as long as the course remains safe and accessible for everyone.

About Active Minds

Active Minds is the leading nonprofit empowering students and young adults to speak openly about mental health, educate their peers, and encourage help-seeking.

Through a nationwide network of campus chapters, awareness campaigns, and youth leadership programs, Active Minds is transforming how mental health is talked about, valued, and supported in schools and communities across the country.

By participating in the Run for Hope 5K, you’re helping expand conversations around mental health, reduce stigma, and create more supportive environments for students at the University of Alabama and beyond.

FAQ

Who can participate?

Students, alumni, parents, staff, and community members are all welcome to join the Run for Hope 5K.

Can I walk instead of run?

Yes. Walkers are absolutely welcome. This event is about showing up and supporting mental health.

Do I receive a T-shirt?

Yes, every registered participant who completes the $25 Stripe checkout will receive an event T-shirt while supplies last.

Where is the start and finish?

The 5K starts and finishes at the University of Alabama Student Recreation Center.

What happens if it rains?

The event will take place rain or shine, unless severe weather makes conditions unsafe. Any updates will be emailed to registered participants.
